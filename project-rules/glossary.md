# Industrial Heat Exchange Platform
## Project Glossary

### Heat Source
A facility that produces excess thermal energy as a byproduct of its operations.

Examples:
- factories
- power plants
- data centers
- industrial manufacturing facilities

The system estimates the amount of waste heat available for recovery from these sources.

### Heat Consumer
A building or facility that can use recovered heat for operational needs.

Examples:
- greenhouses
- apartment buildings
- hospitals
- laundries
- food processing plants
- district heating networks

Consumers have thermal demand that can potentially be met by recovered heat.

### Waste Heat
Thermal energy produced during industrial processes that is not currently used and is released into the environment.

Examples:
- exhaust gases
- cooling water
- industrial furnaces
- server cooling systems

Waste heat represents a potential energy recovery opportunity.

### Recoverable Heat
The portion of waste heat that can realistically be captured and reused.

Recoverable heat depends on:
- temperature of the heat source
- technical feasibility
- heat recovery efficiency
- distance to the consumer

Recoverable heat is typically a fraction of total waste heat.

### Heat Exchange Opportunity
A potential pairing between a heat source and a heat consumer that could allow recovered heat to be transferred.

Each opportunity is evaluated using:
- distance
- recoverable heat
- infrastructure cost
- economic value

### Infrastructure Cost
The estimated cost required to build the heat transfer system connecting a heat source and a heat consumer.

Infrastructure may include:
- insulated pipelines
- heat exchangers
- pumps
- valves and control systems
- installation and integration

Infrastructure cost is one of the primary factors used in the financial model.

### Heat Transfer Distance
The distance between a heat source and a heat consumer.

Distance affects:
- pipeline cost
- heat loss
- overall feasibility

Shorter distances generally result in lower infrastructure costs and higher feasibility.

### Energy Recovery
The amount of thermal energy captured and delivered to a consumer from a heat source.

Usually measured in:
- MWh per year

Energy recovery is used to estimate:
- financial savings
- emission reductions
- project viability

### Annual Energy Savings
The financial value of the recovered heat replacing conventional heating energy.

Annual savings are calculated using:
- Recovered Energy × Energy Price

Energy prices may be estimated from:
- natural gas prices
- industrial heat costs
- regional energy benchmarks

### Payback Period
The time required for the savings from recovered heat to offset the infrastructure cost.

Formula:
- Payback Period = Infrastructure Cost / Annual Savings

Shorter payback periods indicate more economically attractive projects.

### Emissions Reduction
The estimated reduction in greenhouse gas emissions resulting from replacing fossil fuel heating with recovered heat.

Usually measured in:
- tons of CO₂ per year

Emission reductions are calculated using an emission factor per unit of energy.

### Feasibility Score
A composite score representing the overall viability of a heat exchange opportunity.

The score incorporates:
- infrastructure cost
- annual savings
- distance
- recoverable heat
- technical constraints

The feasibility score allows the platform to rank opportunities and highlight the most promising projects.

### Opportunity Ranking
A sorted list of heat exchange opportunities based on their feasibility score.

Higher ranked opportunities indicate:
- stronger economic value
- lower infrastructure cost
- higher environmental benefit

### Financial Model
A calculation system used to estimate:
- infrastructure cost
- energy recovery
- annual savings
- payback period

The financial model provides the economic analysis for each opportunity.

### Environmental Impact
The estimated reduction in carbon emissions and energy waste achieved by implementing a heat exchange opportunity.

Environmental impact metrics include:
- CO₂ reduction
- energy recovered
- fossil fuel displacement

### Heat Network
A system of pipelines and equipment used to transport thermal energy from a heat source to one or more consumers.

Heat networks are commonly used in:
- district heating systems
- industrial symbiosis networks

## Controlled Vocabulary Rules

Use these exact terms:
- Heat Source
- Heat Consumer
- Waste Heat
- Recoverable Heat
- Infrastructure Cost
- Annual Energy Savings
- Emissions Reduction
- Feasibility Score

Avoid alternative terms such as:
- Provider instead of Heat Source
- Receiver instead of Heat Consumer
- Viability Index instead of Feasibility Score
